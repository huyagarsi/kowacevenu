FAQs about Eugene Istomin

Who was Eugene Istomin?

He was an acclaimed American pianist known for his virtuosity, poetic insight, and distinctive style. He gained international recognition as a soloist, orchestral performer, and chamber musician.

What are some notable achievements in Istomin’s early career?

At the age of 17, he won both the Leventritt and Philadelphia Orchestra Youth Awards, making his notable debuts with the Philadelphia Orchestra and the New York Philharmonic in the same week of 1943.

What was the Istomin-Stern-Rose Trio?

Formed in 1961 with violinist Isaac Stern and cellist Leonard Rose, this trio became one of the most esteemed chamber ensembles, celebrated for their recordings of Beethoven, Brahms, and Schubert.

What awards did Istomin win?

Among various honors, he won a Grammy Award in 1970 with the Istomin-Stern-Rose Trio for their recordings of Beethoven.

Did he have any unique performance practices?

Known for his dedication to high standards, Istomin embarked on a notable tour in the 1980s, carrying his own Steinway pianos across 30 cities in a specially fitted truck, underscoring his belief that classical music should be accessible everywhere.

How did he contribute to music education and culture?

He was actively involved in cultural diplomacy, performing at significant events under several U.S. presidents and at venues like the White House and United Nations.

What were some significant recordings by Eugene Istomin?

He made many influential recordings, including concertos and solo works. His recording of Bach’s D minor Concerto with the Busch Chamber Players was particularly acclaimed.

Did he premiere any notable works?

Yes, he premiered several works written for him by composers like Roger Sessions, Henri Dutilleux, and Ned Rorem.

What personal interests did Istomin have outside of music?

Besides his musical career, Istomin had a deep interest in reading, book collecting, and sports, particularly baseball.

When and where did Istomin die?

He passed away on October 10, 2003, in Washington, D.C., from liver cancer.

Was he recognized with any international honors?

He was awarded the French Légion d’Honneur in 2001, reflecting his significant impact on music and culture internationally.

Who was Istomin married to?

He married Marta Montañez Martinez, the widow of famed cellist Pablo Casals, in 1975.
